# Inventory Strategy and Warehouse Capacity Optimization
[View Google Sheet](https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/1T2DTTSv_rbLaYsBJGA7yLAdoZhkDrVee94ZtjZ9250w/edit?usp=sharing)

## Overview
Developed an inventory planning model for 100+ SKUs to optimize stock levels while maintaining target service levels. The analysis incorporated demand variability, safety stock, and warehouse capacity constraints.

## Objective
- Estimate SKU-level demand and variability  
- Calculate safety stock based on service levels and lead time  
- Evaluate warehouse space and pallet requirements  
- Assess feasibility of inventory plan  

## Methodology
- Calculated mean and standard deviation using recent demand data  
- Applied safety stock formula using Z-score and lead time  
- Computed new inventory levels and storage requirements  
- Converted warehouse space into pallet requirements  

## Key Insight
The inventory plan required 3,270 pallets, exceeding available warehouse capacity of 2,000 pallets, indicating a 63% capacity shortfall.

## Recommendations
- Increase warehouse capacity  
  Disadvantage: high cost and long-term commitment  

- Reduce service levels for low-priority SKUs  
  Disadvantage: higher risk of stockouts  

- Apply ABC inventory prioritization  
  Disadvantage: uneven service levels across products  

## Tools
- Excel / Google Sheets  
- Statistical analysis (mean, standard deviation)  
- Inventory modeling (safety stock, service levels)  

## Outcome
Identified a critical warehouse constraint and proposed data-driven strategies to balance service level and storage capacity.
